About Me

Howdy! My name is Caroline Mejia and I am a senior Computer Engineering major at Texas A&M University. I have completed a minor in Mathematics and I hope to complete a minor in Cybersecurity. At Texas A&M University, I am a member of the Society of Women in Engineering and the Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers. In my spare time, I enjoy playing video games such as Valorant, Portal, Minecraft, and I especially love playing any Legend of Zelda game (Majora's Mask is currently my favorite game in the series). When I am not investing my time in video games, I like to take road trips and explore different restaurants along the way. I would say that I could be a foodie, however, I would not consider myself to be a food expert; if you ever need a restaurant recommendation I can provide that! I have found that through food, you are able to truly uncover what makes culture so great. I pride myself on exploring new cuisine, however, the cuisine of my own heritage will always be my favorite. I am Latina through my mother and father, although I do have Puerto Rican influences from my step-grandfather. My mother is Mexican; she was born and raised in a small town named Santiago de Ixcuintla and she eventually moved to the US when she married my father. My dad is Ecuadorean and he was born in Manta, Ecuador. At the young age of 7, my dad moved to Brooklyn, New York, and has lived in the US ever since. Although it might not be very common, I am very proud to be a Latina woman pursuing a degree in the STEM field. My dad, being an electrical engineer, inspired me to want to go into the engineering field. Now here I am 1 year away from obtaining a Bachelor's Degree. I am excited to see what the future holds and I hope you will join me on my journey!
